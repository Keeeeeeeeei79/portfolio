Peer Evaluation · Reviewed by Minghong Zhang
Peer Review: One of the Most Professionalised ePortfolios in Our Group

"What immediately strikes about the portfolio of Kei is that it has been designed beautifully with its dark and clean design, the design is professional and modern and every page has a similar stylistic appearance. It is also obvious that much consideration has been given to the aesthetics and the user experience, the user is guided by the intuitive navigation and the content flows naturally one page to another."

"What impressed me the most is how Kei has made the various experiences he has had, working as a physiotherapist in Japan and a barista in Auckland, into a coherent career narrative. It is not that he is trying to make these experiences seem unrelated to each other, but instead the portfolio shows the viewer a well-planned, personalized, and intentional way to a career in data analytics."

"The reflective journals are well considered and reflect a true sense of self-awareness. Kei does not merely recount what he did learn, he elaborates on how the learnings have transformed his thinking and approaches to working with colleagues and different cultures. The teamwork and cross cultural communication section was especially good as it is based on actual experiences and not mere theory."

"The CV is properly formatted and clearly displays his qualifications and experience, the learning goals section indicates that he has a systematic approach to his professional development, which has clearly defined, measurable goals indicating forward planning."

"The only thing I would recommend, as the portfolio evolves, is to put some real-world examples of the data analytics work or projects in place, as it would add a new layer and demonstrate his technical competency, in addition to the already well-developed reflective and professional material."

"On balance, the portfolio of Kei is one of the most professionalised and best organised ePortfolios I have ever viewed within our group. It is professional and personal and it is obvious that Kei does not underestimate his career development."

Minghong Zhang, ICL Classmate

My Response & Next Steps
01
Continue refining the visual design and consistency across all portfolio pages, maintaining the professional standard Minghong highlighted.
02
Add concrete data analytics project examples as I develop my technical skills — visualisations, case studies, and dataset analyses.
03
Keep strengthening the connection between my diverse career experiences and my future goals in data analytics throughout the portfolio.
04
Regularly seek peer feedback and treat the portfolio as a living document that evolves with my professional development.
