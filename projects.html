Portfolio 01
Cafe Sales Analysis Dashboard
Data Analytics · ICL
Context

As a part of my data analytics training experience at ICL, I completed an independent project to analyse the data on sales of the cafes and create a visual dashboard that could assist in the business decision-making process in the real hospitality setting.

Objective

Develop a dynamic dashboard to visualise sales patterns per month, menu item performance, and the busiest trading days — offer practical guidance to the cafe management.

My Role & Approach

My responsibility was limited to sourcing, cleaning, analyzing and designing the dashboard with data. I gathered and sorted raw sales data, used pivot tables to find the trends, and developed visual charts such as the sales trends by month, menu breakdown by category and the peak hour analysis. I also worked on ensuring that the dashboard is easy to use and understand by non technical stakeholders.

Outcome

It was evident in the dashboard that the highest revenue-generating times were weekday mornings and that some menu items were performing much better than others. Such analysis can assist in optimising staffing, inventory and promotional plans of the cafe managers.

Portfolio 02
Xero Organisational Behaviour Essay
Contemporary Leadership & Management (9230) · ICL
Context

In the Contemporary Leadership and Management course at ICL, I had to critically scrutinise the behaviour, culture and approach of a real organisation in managing change using well-known academic frameworks.

Objective

Assess the organisational culture at Xero, the motivation strategies used, and how it handles change management — connecting analysis to appropriate management theories.

My Role & Approach

It was a personal research and scholarly writing paper. I used AMO Theory (Ability, Motivation, Opportunity) to examine how Xero empowers its staff by developing their skills, intrinsic motivation, and a culture of innovation. I also applied Kotter's 8-Step Change Model to assess how Xero coped with the transition of a local New Zealand based accounting software company to a global SaaS platform, looking at communication within the leadership, coalition building, and embedding of new organisational practices.

Outcome

The essay has shown that the key factors that have contributed to the success of Xero would be people-centred culture and systematic approach to organisational change. I have got great feedback on the ability to connect theory to the real-life business practice.

Framework
AMO Theory
Model
Kotter 8-Step
Type
Academic Essay
